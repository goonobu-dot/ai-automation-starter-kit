Work Relief Workflows: Five Email-Free Review Packs

This manual covers five local, human-approved packs for administrative relief work: Handover and deadline review, Employee lifecycle checks, Contract and application document checks, Quote comparison and pre-purchase review, and Compliance and license deadline tracking. Every pack stays inside a local folder workflow, a localhost UI, and a PIN approval gate. Email automation is out of scope.

Local first

Files stay on the operator machine during inspection, drafting, and approval.

Human last word

Codex drafts and asks questions. A named approver reviews and approves the final local copy.

Email-free scope

Gmail and email automation are out of scope for these packs and will be handled as a separate project.

1. What this route is for

Read this before you start

These five packs reduce repetitive review work. They do not send mail, update Gmail, reply to customers, file legal documents, correct HR records, issue approvals on their own, or make production changes. Gmail and email automation will be a separate project.

Good fit

You already receive files in folders or exports, the final output follows a known structure, and a responsible person can review the result.

Bad fit

The work depends on live inbox activity, direct email sending, customer promises, payroll mutation, legal submission, or any irreversible external action.

What you get

A local workspace, a pack-specific review loop, AI clarification questions, a draft, a PIN approval record, and a prepared next-day folder.

3. Choose the right pack

Pack Use it when Main current-period source Human-only decision
Handover and Deadline Review You need a clean daily handover and a clear list of changed, at-risk, or blocked deadlines. Task board export, handover notes, deadline change note Customer-visible promise, final owner, actual deadline change
Employee Lifecycle Checklist You need to check onboarding, transfer, leave, return, or offboarding steps without changing HR systems. Employee status change export, checklist tracker, manager note HR judgment, record correction, payroll or access change
Contract and Application Completeness You need to confirm whether intake packets are complete before a human review. Contract or application register, packet index, completeness note Legal or compliance interpretation, filing, signature, acceptance
Quote Comparison and Pre-Purchase Review You need a structured comparison before someone buys, signs, or places an order. Quote comparison sheet, purchase request, evaluation note Supplier selection, commitment, purchase approval, payment
Compliance and License Deadline Tracking You need a local queue of renewals, evidence deadlines, and escalation candidates. Deadline register, license inventory, owner update note Renewal submission, compliance sign-off, external filing
Simple selection rule

Pick the pack by the decision boundary, not the department name. If the pack ends in a checklist, comparison, or deadline queue and a human still owns the final action, it belongs here. If it ends in sending mail, editing Gmail, or auto-issuing messages, it does not.

5. Walk through the localhost UI

Start the local operator UI 
ai-automation-kit office-workspace serve \
  --root "$HOME/AI-Office-Workspaces" \
  --language en
1. Create the workspace

Choose the work relief pack, enter the first day in YYYY-MM-DD, name the workspace, set the approver name, and create a local approval PIN.

2. Open the folders

Open 01_APPROVED_PAST_OUTPUTS, 02_PAST_SUPPORTING_FILES, and 03_CURRENT_INPUTS/<day> from the folder panel so the operator sees the right drop zones.

3. Inspect materials

Select Inspect materials. Check accepted, rejected, and skipped files before you move forward.

4. Answer AI questions

The pack asks only for missing policy choices or operating boundaries that the files do not answer cleanly.

5. Generate the draft

Select Generate draft. The draft appears under 05_DRAFTS/<day> for review.

6. Review the draft

Check names, dates, quantities, owners, deadlines, missing evidence, and anything that could create a business promise or compliance error.

7. Approve with PIN

The named approver enters the stored name and PIN. The approved file moves to 06_APPROVED_OUTPUTS/<day> and the audit trail updates locally.

Pack Typical AI questions What the human must answer carefully
Handover and Deadline Review What counts as high-risk delay? Which owner takes unresolved items today? Any customer-visible deadline change, escalation rule, or exception approval
Employee Lifecycle Checklist Which checklist items must be complete the same day? Which steps require manager confirmation? HR exceptions, payroll effect, access enable or disable decision
Contract and Application Completeness What fields are mandatory? What is the cutoff for “complete enough for review”? Legal interpretation, acceptance, filing, or signature decision
Quote Comparison and Pre-Purchase Review What is the approval threshold? Which missing detail blocks purchase review? Supplier selection, purchase decision, price exception, commitment
Compliance and License Deadline Tracking How many days before deadline counts as urgent? Which licenses must escalate same day? Renewal approval, external filing, compliance sign-off, legal escalation

6. Reuse the pack the next day

Prepare next day

After approval, use the UI to prepare the next day. Confirm the suggested date before the new folder is created.

Reuse the approved example

The latest approved output can be reused as a trusted style and structure reference for the next run.

Keep inputs clean

Put only the new day’s files into the next 03_CURRENT_INPUTS/<day> folder. Do not mix days.

How trusted prior outputs are protected

The reused example must come from an approved local output in the same workspace. Its recorded hash is checked again before the next draft run. A changed or substituted file is rejected instead of silently reused.

What reuse does not mean

Reuse is not permanent model training. It is a bounded reference to an approved local example inside the next isolated run. The new day still depends on the new day’s files.

7. Troubleshooting

Codex is not ready

Run codex login status. Finish the local login first. Do not paste an API key into the browser or this workflow.

The UI opens but the pack is not usable

Refresh the workspace list, confirm the first day format is YYYY-MM-DD, and confirm that the workspace was created under the root you served.

Files were rejected or skipped

Check the extension, corruption, and actual placement. Put current files in 03_CURRENT_INPUTS/<day>, not in a parent folder.

The draft is thin or misses facts

Add the missing current file or reference document, inspect again, answer the follow-up questions, and regenerate. Do not approve a draft that guesses.

The approver cannot approve

Confirm the approver name and PIN match the workspace setup. Approval is local to this workspace and this device.

The next day does not show the right prior example

Confirm the current day is approved and the approved file has not changed after approval. The next run will reject a tampered example.

The run stopped halfway

Refresh the workspace state first. If manual recovery is required, inspect 00_START_HERE/workspace_status.json, .system/workspace.json, the current cycle state file, unfinished runs, and 07_AUDIT/audit.jsonl.

8. Safety boundaries

Codex may

Read supported local files, organize facts, ask for missing policy choices, produce a local draft, and reference a trusted prior approved output.

Codex may not

Send email, update Gmail, message vendors, change deadlines in production systems, alter HR records, submit compliance filings, pay invoices, or approve its own output.

The human must

Choose the correct files, answer the business questions, verify the draft, enter the approval PIN, and decide what happens outside the local workspace.

Explicit scope line

Gmail and email automation are out of scope for this manual and will be a separate project. Do not sell or describe these packs as inbox automation.

9. Deliver it as a bounded side business

1. Sell one pack

Pick one pack with obvious inputs, one clear approver, and one measurable review bottleneck.

2. Use sanitized samples

Ask for masked or approved examples first. Build the demonstration with safe local data.

3. Show the boundary

Explain exactly where AI stops: draft, checklist, queue, or comparison. The client keeps the final operational action.

4. Measure the boring work

Track review minutes saved, missing-item rate, corrections per draft, and approval cycle time.

5. Keep Gmail separate

If the client later wants inbox automation, proposal sending, or follow-up emails, scope that as a separate project with separate controls.

Do not promise

Do not promise fully autonomous back office operation, automatic legal compliance, automatic HR completion, automatic purchasing, or automatic email handling. Promise a local review loop with a visible approval boundary.
