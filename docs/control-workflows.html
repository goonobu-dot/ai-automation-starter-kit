Control Workflow Packs: Beginner Operating Manual

Use six daily control packs to turn messy office evidence into local review drafts. This manual covers install, Codex login, exact folders, localhost operation, source review, AI questions, draft filenames, PIN approval, next-day reuse, troubleshooting, safety, and side-business positioning.

The six workflows are Spreadsheet reconciliation, Policy change impact, Quality incident and corrective action, Vendor onboarding, Access review, and Grant application packet preparation.

1. What this manual is for

Human-first boundary: these packs create local drafts for review. They do not approve controls, post finance entries, submit grants, change access, activate vendors, close CAPAs, send email, or contact anyone outside your computer.

The six packs are for daily control work where the expensive part is collecting evidence, comparing it, identifying missing facts, and preparing a sourced draft for a responsible person. They are not full automation. The useful promise is smaller and safer: reduce copy-paste and first-draft time while keeping approval, judgment, and external action with a human.

Best fitTeams that already have recurring files, a named approver, and a current manual review process.
Bad fitTeams asking AI to certify compliance, authorize payments, change access, or make legal decisions without review.
Core rhythmPlace files, inspect sources, answer questions, generate a draft, review facts, approve locally, reuse tomorrow.

Email automation is out of scope. Gmail, Outlook, Slack, Teams, vendor notifications, applicant notifications, customer updates, and any external message sending are separate projects. This manual deliberately keeps the control packs local and review-only.

2. The visual daily flow

1. Choose packSelect one of the six control packs and enter the first date as YYYY-MM-DD.
2. Add sourcesPut required files in the exact workspace folders. Keep current-day material separate.
3. InspectCheck accepted, skipped, rejected, and missing source records before drafting.
4. AnswerGive the AI the control threshold, escalation rule, and any hold rule.
5. DraftGenerate a sourced local markdown draft in 05_DRAFTS.
6. ReviewCompare the draft against source files. Mark unsupported claims before approval.
7. ApproveUse approver name and PIN to copy the reviewed file into 06_APPROVED_OUTPUTS.

5. Create a workspace

The localhost UI is the easiest route for beginners because it shows the pack list and date format. Start the UI with:

ai-automation-kit office-workspace serve --root "$HOME/AI-Office-Workspaces" --language en

The terminal prints an address like http://127.0.0.1:8080/. Open that address on the same computer. Keep the server on 127.0.0.1; it is not a public web app.

You can also create a workspace directly from the CLI. This example creates a spreadsheet reconciliation workspace for July 13, 2026:

ai-automation-kit office-workspace create \
  --root ./workspaces \
  --name "Client A Spreadsheet Reconciliation" \
  --approver "Finance Manager" \
  --pin 482913 \
  --period 2026-07-13 \
  --pack spreadsheet-reconciliation-daily \
  --language en

ai-automation-kit office-workspace status \
  --workspace ./workspaces/Client_A_Spreadsheet_Reconciliation \
  --json

The PIN is local approval protection. It is not a bank PIN, device PIN, account password, or secret to paste into a client chat. Use a temporary workflow PIN and store it according to the client's internal rules.

6. Exact folders and what goes inside

00_START_HERERead the local status notes first. This folder helps a new operator understand the workspace state.
01_APPROVED_PAST_OUTPUTSPut older approved final outputs here. Use them as style and structure examples, not as current facts.
02_PAST_SUPPORTING_FILESPut standing references here: policies, matrices, templates, approved control language, prior notes, and definitions.
03_CURRENT_INPUTS/YYYY-MM-DDPut today's source evidence here. For July 13, 2026, use 03_CURRENT_INPUTS/2026-07-13.
04_QUESTIONS/YYYY-MM-DDGenerated missing-information questions appear here when the source review is not enough.
05_DRAFTS/YYYY-MM-DDDraft markdown files appear here. These are not approved deliverables.
06_APPROVED_OUTPUTS/YYYY-MM-DDThe reviewed file is copied here only after approver name and PIN approval.
07_AUDITLocal audit records and approval evidence live here. Do not edit these by hand during normal operation.

Input examples by pack

spreadsheet-reconciliation-daily

Put ledger_export_2026-07-13.csv, bank_statement_2026-07-13.xlsx, and optional reconciliation_policy.md in the current date folder or supporting folders. The draft should list matched totals, unmatched items, missing owners, cutoff issues, and items that need escalation. It must not post journal entries, adjust balances, pay, or sign off reconciliation.

policy-change-impact-daily

Use files such as policy_notice_vendor_security.pdf, control_inventory.xlsx, and optional exception_log.csv. The draft should map the notice to affected controls, processes, training, approvals, and open questions. It must not publish policies, change controls, assign training, or request attestations.

quality-incident-capa-daily

Use quality_incident_log.csv, capa_tracker.xlsx, and optional audit notes. The draft should identify severity, containment owner, due date, verification status, overdue CAPAs, and missing evidence. It must not close CAPAs, change product disposition, authorize release, or notify suppliers.

vendor-onboarding-daily

Use vendor_intake_register.xlsx, due diligence PDFs, tax notes, and banking setup notes. The draft should separate ready, blocked, and missing-evidence vendors. It must not activate vendors, accept tax forms, update banking, pay vendors, or contact vendors.

access-review-daily

Use access_review_export.csv, role_entitlement_matrix.xlsx, and optional joiner-mover-leaver logs. The draft should identify privileged access, mismatched roles, missing approval evidence, and exceptions. It must not grant access, revoke access, certify a review, or contact users.

grant-packet-daily

Use grant_notice.pdf, eligibility_budget_workbook.xlsx, and supporting attachments. The draft should organize eligibility evidence, budget support, narrative support, attachment status, deadlines, and unresolved questions. It must not submit anything, certify eligibility, sign forms, or promise an award.

7. Operate the workflow

Inspect sources first

After placing files, click Inspect materials in the UI or run:

ai-automation-kit office-workspace inspect \
  --workspace ./workspaces/Client_A_Spreadsheet_Reconciliation \
  --period 2026-07-13

Review accepted, rejected, and skipped records. Confirm the source manifest includes the files you expected. If a required input is missing, do not draft around it. Add the missing file or answer that the evidence is unavailable.

Answer AI questions

The AI questions are not decoration. They define how the draft should interpret risk and materiality. Good answers are short, concrete, and owned by the responsible person.

  • For reconciliation: "Escalate any unreconciled item above USD 500, any bank fee without owner, and any same-day cutoff mismatch."
  • For policy impact: "Material impact means the change affects a customer-facing control, regulated process, training obligation, or executive sign-off."
  • For CAPA: "Critical quality risk means open containment for shipped product, overdue verification, repeat defect, or missing root cause owner."
  • For vendors: "Minimum readiness requires legal name, tax form evidence, sanctions screen, payment terms, owner approval, and banking evidence."
  • For access review: "Privileged scope includes admin, production write, finance approval, security console, and shared owner accounts."
  • For grants: "Eligibility must be supported by the notice, applicant records, budget workbook, and attachment evidence; unknown means unresolved."

Generate, then read the draft against sources

When the UI shows the run is ready, generate the draft. The expected draft filenames are exact: spreadsheet_reconciliation_daily.md, policy_change_impact_daily.md, quality_incident_capa_daily.md, vendor_onboarding_daily.md, access_review_daily.md, and grant_packet_daily.md.

Open the file in 05_DRAFTS/YYYY-MM-DD. Check every number, date, person, system, vendor, policy, program, deadline, amount, and recommendation against the source files. Unsupported claims should be removed or marked as missing evidence before approval.

Approve with PIN only after review

Approval copies the reviewed draft into 06_APPROVED_OUTPUTS/YYYY-MM-DD and records audit information. Approval does not send, publish, submit, update production, or notify anyone. The responsible human still decides whether the approved local file can be used outside the workspace.

8. Reuse the workspace tomorrow

After approval, prepare the next date. Daily packs use calendar dates, so July 13, 2026 rolls to 2026-07-14. The prior approved output can be reused as a style and structure reference when the UI offers it.

  1. Confirm today's approved output is the one you want tomorrow to follow.
  2. Prepare the next period in the UI or by using the workspace controls.
  3. Place only the new day's source files in 03_CURRENT_INPUTS/2026-07-14.
  4. Keep standing policies and matrices in 02_PAST_SUPPORTING_FILES.
  5. Inspect again. The system should not assume yesterday's facts are today's facts.
  6. Answer new questions if policy, threshold, approver, or hold rules changed.
  7. Generate, source-review, approve, and record the next local output.
Reuse is not training: the system uses the prior approved output as a checked reference for structure and tone. It does not permanently train a model on your files.

9. Safety rules for control work

Never skip source reviewA clean-looking draft can still be wrong. Compare it to the accepted source manifest and original files.
Never approve your own high-risk outputUse the named finance, compliance, quality, procurement, security, IT, grant, or finance approver.
Never turn drafts into external actionsEmail automation, access changes, vendor activation, payment, grant submission, and policy publication are outside this pack family.
Never hide uncertaintyIf evidence is missing, the draft should say so. Do not let the AI invent eligibility, approval, or root-cause facts.

For real client or company data, start with sanitized samples. If the workflow handles regulated, personal, financial, security, medical, legal, export-controlled, or grant-certification material, the responsible organization must decide whether local AI review is allowed.

10. Troubleshooting

The UI does not open

Confirm the terminal running office-workspace serve is still active. Open the exact 127.0.0.1 URL printed by the command. If the port is busy, restart the server and use the new printed address.

Codex is not logged in

Run codex login status. Complete local login. Do not paste an API key, password, or token into the manual, UI, or chat.

The date is rejected

Use strict YYYY-MM-DD. Examples: 2026-07-13 and 2026-12-01. Invalid dates, month-only dates, and informal labels are rejected for these daily packs.

A file is skipped or rejected

Check extension, corruption, size, and whether the file is a shortcut or cloud placeholder. The packs accept combinations of .csv, .xlsx, .json, .pdf, .docx, .md, and .txt, depending on the input slot.

The AI asks too many questions

That usually means the files do not contain enough evidence. Add a policy, matrix, owner list, deadline, threshold, or prior approved output. Do not answer with guesses when the organization has a real rule.

The draft has confident but unsupported text

Remove the claim, add the source, or mark the issue unresolved. Approval should mean the human reviewer has compared the claim to evidence.

Approval fails

Check approver spelling, PIN, current period, and whether the draft is the current draft for that date. Do not manually copy files into 06_APPROVED_OUTPUTS as a workaround.

Manual recovery is required

Check 00_START_HERE/workspace_status.json, .system/workspace.json, .system/periods/YYYY-MM-DD/state.json, .system/runs/, and 07_AUDIT/audit.jsonl. Stop and summarize the known state before editing anything by hand.

11. Side-business positioning

The strongest beginner offer is not "I will automate compliance." The safer offer is: "I will set up one local daily control-draft workspace, prove it on sample data, and leave approval with your responsible person." This is easier to sell honestly because the buyer can see the folders, source manifest, questions, draft, approval record, and limits.

A practical first paid pilot

  1. Choose one buyer role: finance manager, compliance owner, quality manager, procurement lead, IT/security lead, or grants administrator.
  2. Ask for one painful daily review that already has files and a named approver.
  3. Use sanitized sample data for the demo and avoid live systems.
  4. Measure draft time, missing evidence count, correction count, and final review time.
  5. Deliver the workspace, this manual, a one-page scope, and a list of actions that remain human-only.
  6. Price the pilot as setup plus one or two review cycles, not as a permanent autonomous agent.
Do not claim full automation: do not promise guaranteed accuracy, automatic control approval, audit certification, automatic access change, vendor activation, grant submission, payment, or external communication. The product value is controlled drafting and repeatable review.
